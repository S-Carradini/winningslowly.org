Of Popes and Philadelphians

  • February 7, 2014
  • Topics: art, Christianity, economics
  • Download: M4A (for iTunes) or MP3

Show Notes

What happens when Pope Francis goes to Philadelphia and drop-kicks Mario in the face? Will the internet explode at all the coolness?

Just how amazing was the Pope's trip to Philadelphia? Well, we could go on and on, waxing ever more eloquent about the sorts of things one might go all googley-eyed for when a pope visits a new city... or we could just list them off. By which I mean: here's an excuse to have had a long paragraph, and now we get to use an ordered list. Important stuff, folks.

  1. He ate a Philly cheese-steak. Of course. He didn't much like it.
  2. He climbed on top of the Liberty Bell.
  3. He proclaimed that Philadelphia would henceforth be known as Philadelphstar, and no one knows why yet.
